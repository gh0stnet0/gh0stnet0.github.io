2024.03.09 iot infra

IoT recon methodology
passive fingerprinting at scale

Most IoT devices are invisible to their owners and loud to everyone else. This is about listening before anyone knows you're there.

the problem with IoT

The average network has more IoT devices than its operator can name. Cameras, routers, sensors, controllers — most running firmware from 2019, most with default credentials, most broadcasting their presence to anyone willing to listen.

Passive fingerprinting doesn't knock on doors. It watches who leaves their lights on.

phase 01 — network discovery

Before touching a single packet, map the terrain passively. ARP traffic alone tells you more than most active scans.

# passive ARP monitoring — no packets sent
tcpdump -i eth0 -n arp 2>/dev/null | tee arp_capture.log
 
# extract unique IPs from capture
awk '{print $NF}' arp_capture.log | sort -u
 
# cross-reference with OUI database for vendor
grep -f mac_list.txt oui_database.txt
 
→ 192.168.1.47 b8:27:eb:xx:xx:xx Raspberry Pi Foundation
→ 192.168.1.103 d8:3a:dd:xx:xx:xx Espressif Systems (ESP32)
→ 192.168.1.201 00:12:fb:xx:xx:xx Samsung Electronics

The OUI prefix alone reveals vendor. Vendor reveals device category. Device category reveals probable firmware, probable ports, probable vulnerabilities. You haven't sent a single probe yet.

phase 02 — traffic behavior analysis

IoT devices are creatures of habit. They beacon at fixed intervals, connect to the same endpoints, use the same protocols. Behavioral fingerprinting is more reliable than banner grabbing.

# capture traffic patterns for specific host
tcpdump -i eth0 host 192.168.1.103 -w device_traffic.pcap
 
# analyze connection intervals and destinations
tshark -r device_traffic.pcap -T fields \
-e frame.time_delta -e ip.dst -e tcp.dstport \
| sort | uniq -c | sort -rn | head -20
 
→ beacons every 30s to 52.84.x.x:443 (AWS IoT Core)
→ NTP sync every 3600s to pool.ntp.org
→ mDNS broadcast every 5s on 224.0.0.251
note: mDNS broadcasts are gold. Devices announce their services, hostnames and capabilities to the local network without being asked. Listen on 224.0.0.251:5353 and they introduce themselves.

phase 03 — service fingerprinting

Only after passive observation do we move to active probing — and even then, we keep it surgical. Banner grabbing on known ports, nothing that generates anomalous traffic patterns.

# targeted service grab — known ports only
nmap -sV -p 80,443,8080,8443,1883,5683 \
--version-intensity 3 192.168.1.103
 
PORT STATE SERVICE VERSION
1883/tcp open mqtt Mosquitto 2.0.11
8080/tcp open http lighttpd 1.4.55
 
# grab http headers without browser fingerprint
curl -sI http://192.168.1.103:8080 | grep -i "server\|x-powered"

what you end up with

After passive + surgical active recon, you have a complete device profile:

  • Vendor and device model via OUI + mDNS
  • Firmware version via service banners
  • Cloud endpoints and communication patterns
  • Open services and their versions
  • Behavioral baseline for anomaly detection

All of this before the device — or its owner — knows you were looking.

scope reminder: this methodology applies to infrastructure you own or have explicit authorization to assess. gh0stnet operates within defined scope. always.
